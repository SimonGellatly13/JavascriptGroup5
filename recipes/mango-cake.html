Mango Cake

Try this Mango Cake recipe with a perfect balance of flavors that you will surely love. Packed with fresh mango goodness from the filling all the way to the toppings. Yum!

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 sponge cake
  • 3 cups ripe mango, cubed (about 1 cm in size)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 10 fluid ounces all-purpose cream (or heavy cream/whipping cream)
  • 7 ounces mascarpone
    (if unavailable, substitute with a total of 2 cups all-purpose cream)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons gelatin powder, dissolved in 1/3 cup cold water
  • 1 cup powdered sugar

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Make the mango purée: Combine 2 cups of ripe mango, 1/2 cup water, and 2 tablespoons sugar. Blend using a blender or stick mixer for about 1 minute, or until completely smooth with no lumps remaining.
  2. Warm the dissolved gelatin in the microwave for 10 seconds or heat gently on the stove until fully melted. Do not let it boil.
  3. Add the melted gelatin and powdered sugar to the mango purée and mix well.
  4. In a large bowl, whip the mascarpone using a hand mixer or stand mixer for 1 minute. While continuously whipping, slowly add the cream and whip until fluffy and stiff peaks form.
  5. Using a spatula, gently fold the mango purée mixture into the whipped cream mixture until fully incorporated.
  6. Assemble the cake: Slice the sponge cake into 2 layers. Place the first layer inside a springform pan or cake ring that is slightly wider than the cake, leaving a 1–2 cm gap around the edges.
  7. Scatter the remaining cubed mango on top of the first cake layer.
  8. Pour enough of the cream mixture to cover the mango cubes completely.
  9. Place the second cake layer on top, then pour the remaining cream mixture over it, spreading to fully cover the cake.
  10. Chill the mango mousse cake in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or until fully set.
  11. Run a warm knife around the edge of the cake to release it from the ring more easily. Decorate as desired and serve cold.
