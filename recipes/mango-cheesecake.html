Mango Cheesecake

Super creamy and smooth Mango Cheesecake with a shortbread cookie crust, topped with sweet, tangy mango curd and slices of fresh mango. It’s like sunshine on a plate!

INGREDIENTS

    CHEESECAKE BASE:
  • 200 g shortbread cookies (makes about 1 1/2 cups crumbs) *
  • 3 tablespoons butter, melted (43 g)
    • FILLING:
  • 1 1/2 pounds cream cheese (680 g)
  • 1 cup sugar (use 1 1/4 cups if mangoes are not very sweet) — 200 g
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch (21 g)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 2 cups mango purée (from about 3 large mangoes) — 15 oz
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
    • TOPPINGS:
  • 3/4 cup mango curd
  • Sliced mangoes, for decorating
  • Mini sugar pearl sprinkles, for decorating

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Adjust the oven rack to the lower-middle position and preheat the oven to 325°F (163°C).
  2. Make the cheesecake base: Pulse the shortbread cookies in a food processor until they become fine crumbs.
  3. Add the melted butter and pulse until the mixture resembles wet sand.
  4. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan.
  5. Bake the crust for about 8 minutes, or until golden and fragrant. Remove from the oven and let cool.
  6. Make the filling: Using a stand mixer with the paddle attachment or a hand mixer, beat the cream cheese until light and fluffy (about 2 minutes). Scrape down the bowl.
  7. Add the sugar and cornstarch and beat for another 2 minutes, scraping the bowl halfway through.
  8. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing on low speed for 20 seconds after each addition. Scrape down the bowl.
  9. Add the mango purée, lime juice, and salt, and mix until fully combined.
  10. Wrap the outside of the springform pan in 2 sheets of heavy-duty aluminum foil to prevent water from entering.
  11. Brush the sides of the pan with butter or spray with nonstick spray.
  12. Pour the cheesecake batter over the prebaked crust and smooth the surface. Tap the pan on the counter several times to release air bubbles.
  13. Place the cheesecake pan inside a larger roasting pan. Fill the larger pan with hot water until it reaches halfway up the sides of the springform pan.
  14. Bake for about 1 1/2 hours, or until the sides are puffy and golden and the center is slightly jiggly.
  15. Place the cheesecake on a cooling rack for 5 minutes, then run a knife around the edges to loosen it. Allow it to cool completely to room temperature (about 3 hours).
  16. Pour the mango curd over the cooled cheesecake and smooth the top.
  17. Wrap the cheesecake tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 6 hours, or until cold and fully set.
  18. To serve: Carefully remove the springform pan sides. Slide a thin metal spatula between the crust and the pan bottom to transfer cheesecake to a serving platter.
  19. If your pan bottom has a raised lip, you may leave it on and place it directly on the serving plate.
  20. Decorate with sliced mango if desired.
