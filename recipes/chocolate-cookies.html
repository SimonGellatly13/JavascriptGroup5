Chocolate Cookies

Soft and chewy chocolate cookies are dense and intensely chocolaty.

INGREDIENTS

  • ¾ cup (170 grams) unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 ½ cup (300 grams) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • ⅔ cup (60 grams) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a sheet pan with parchment paper.
  2. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the butter and sugar on medium-high speed until light and creamy, about 4 minutes.
  3. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing after each addition. Add the vanilla.
  4. In a separate bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt.
  5. Add the dry ingredients to the mixer and mix until just combined. Scrape down the bowl to ensure the batter is evenly mixed.
  6. Scoop dough by the rounded tablespoon (or use a #40 1.5 Tbsp cookie scoop) onto the prepared sheet pan.
  7. Bake until edges are crisp and the tops are just barely set (no longer wet), about 9 minutes. Baking times may vary based on your oven and cookie size.
  8. Immediately sprinkle with coarse sugar or salt while cookies are still hot.
  9. If cookies are misshapen, use a spatula to gently push them back into a circle.
  10. Let cookies cool on the pan for a few minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
  11. Repeat with remaining cookie dough.
  12. Store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for several days.
