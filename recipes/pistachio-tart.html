Pistachio Tart

This triple pistachio tart is a truly decadent dessert that pistachio lovers will adore! It combines a pistachio shortcrust pastry, pistachio sponge and pistachio pastry cream.

INGREDIENTS

    PASTRY DOUGH:
  • 100 g Unsalted Butter, soft, at room temperature
  • 50 g Icing (Powdered) Sugar, sifted
  • 1 Large Egg, at room temperature
  • 40 g Ground Pistachio
  • 200 g All-Purpose Flour
  • 1 pinch Salt
    • PISTACHIO PASTRY CREAM:
  • 250 ml Whole Milk
  • 2 Large Egg Yolks, at room temperature
  • 25 g Caster Sugar
  • 20 g Cornstarch
  • 50 g Pistachio Paste
    • PISTACHIO SPONGE:
  • 100 g Unsalted Butter, soft, at room temperature
  • 100 g Icing (Powdered) Sugar, sifted
  • 2 Large Eggs, at room temperature
  • 120 g Ground Pistachio
  • 20 g Raw Pistachio Kernels
    • TOPPINGS:
  • 20 g Raw Pistachio Kernels, roughly chopped
  • 1 tsp Lime Zest (optional)

INSTRUCTIONS

    PASTRY DOUGH
  1. Place the soft butter and icing sugar in the bowl of your stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment (or a large bowl if using a hand mixer). Mix on medium speed for a couple of minutes or until creamy and smooth.
  2. Mix in the egg and ground pistachio, then the sifted flour and pinch of salt. Slowly mix until a rough paste comes together.
  3. Place the pastry between two sheets of baking paper and slightly flatten it with your hands. Roll into a large disk about 4 mm (1/8 to 1/6 inch) thick.
  4. Place the rolled pastry on a flat tray and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or up to 24 hours.
  5. Take the pastry out of the fridge and let it soften for 5 minutes. Peel off the parchment sheets and transfer into a 22 cm / 9-inch perforated tart ring. Press the edges to fit and trim excess dough.
  6. Loosely cover with baking paper and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
    7. PISTACHIO PASTRY CREAM
  7. Place the milk in a medium saucepan and heat on medium-low until it simmers.
  8. In a heatproof bowl, whisk the egg yolks and sugar until smooth. Add cornstarch and whisk until no lumps remain.
  9. Slowly pour the warm milk over the egg mixture while whisking continuously. Pour the mixture back into the saucepan.
  10. Cook on low heat for about 5 minutes, stirring constantly. When large bubbles appear, whisk vigorously for 30 seconds and remove from heat.
  11. Add pistachio paste and whisk until smooth and shiny.
  12. Transfer to a clean bowl or shallow pan. Cover with plastic wrap touching the surface and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
    13. PAR BAKING THE SHELL
  13. Dock the pastry by pricking the bottom with a fork.
  14. Place in the freezer while preheating the oven to 160°C / 325°F.
  15. Blind bake for 15–20 minutes or until the bottom looks mostly dry. Set aside to cool.
    16. PISTACHIO SPONGE
  16. With the oven still at 160°C / 325°F, place soft butter in a large bowl or stand mixer and mix for 2–3 minutes until very soft and pale.
  17. Add icing sugar and mix until smooth.
  18. Add eggs one at a time, mixing until fully combined.
  19. Add ground pistachio and mix to form a thick, coarse batter.
  20. Pour the sponge mixture into the cooled, par-baked shell. Spread evenly and top with raw pistachios.
  21. Bake for 20–25 minutes or until the sponge is golden brown. Cool completely.
    22. ASSEMBLING THE TART
  22. Whisk the chilled pistachio pastry cream to loosen it. Transfer to a piping bag fitted with a large round tip.
  23. Pipe the pastry cream over the sponge and smooth into an even layer using an offset spatula.
  24. To create a spiral pattern, place the tart on a turntable, place the spatula in the center, and rotate while slowly moving outward.
  25. Finish by sprinkling roughly chopped pistachios around the edges and optionally adding lime zest.
