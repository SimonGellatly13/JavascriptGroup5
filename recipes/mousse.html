Chocolate Mousse

Light and airy yet rich, this is a Chocolate Mousse made the classic French way, as served in fine dining restaurants. Less cream, richer mouth feel, true chocolate flavour. It's actually quite straight forward to make!

INGREDIENTS

    CHOCOLATE MOUSSE:
  • 3 eggs (~55 g / 2 oz each)
  • 125 g (4.5 oz) dark cooking chocolate, bittersweet / 70% cocoa
  • 10 g (0.3 oz) unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup full-fat cream
  • 3 tablespoons caster sugar (superfine sugar)
    • DECORATIONS:
  • Extra whipped cream
  • Chocolate shavings

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. For reliable results, work at a steady pace so your whipped egg whites and cream do not get too warm.
  2. Separate the eggs while they are cold. Place the whites in a large bowl and the yolks in a small bowl. Leave the whites aside while preparing the other ingredients.
  3. Whisk the egg yolks until smooth and uniform.
  4. Melt the chocolate and butter: Break the chocolate into pieces and place them in a microwave-safe bowl with the butter. Melt in 30-second bursts, stirring between each, until smooth. (Add optional flavorings here if using.) Set aside to cool slightly.
  5. Whip the cream: Beat the cream until stiff peaks form. Be careful not to overbeat.
  6. Whip the egg whites: Add sugar to the whites, then beat until firm peaks form.
  7. Fold the ingredients together: Fold the egg yolks into the whipped cream using a rubber spatula — no more than 8 folds. Some streaks are fine.
  8. Check the chocolate temperature: The melted chocolate should be runy and warm — minimum 35°C (95°F), ideally around 40°C (104°F). If too cool or thick, microwave in 5-second bursts until runny.
  9. Pour the warm chocolate into the cream-yolk mixture and fold no more than 8 times. Some streaks are okay.
  10. Add 1/4 of the beaten egg whites to the chocolate mixture. Fold until incorporated — aim for about 10 folds. Smear the spatula across the surface to blend in any white lumps.
  11. Pour the chocolate mixture into the remaining egg whites. Fold gently until fully incorporated and no visible white streaks remain — about 12 folds maximum.
  12. Divide the mixture between 4 small glasses or dessert pots. Refrigerate for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight.
  13. To serve: Garnish with whipped cream and chocolate shavings. Raspberries and a small mint sprig also make a beautiful finishing touch.
