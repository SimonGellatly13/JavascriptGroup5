Strawberry Cake

The one thing that sets this strawberry cake apart from others? Reduce fresh strawberry puree down and add it to the best white cake batter.

INGREDIENTS

    STRAWBERRY PUREE
  • 1 pound (454g) fresh strawberries, rinsed and hulled
    • CAKE
  • 2 and 1/2 cups (295g) cake flour (spooned & leveled)
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup (12 Tbsp; 170g) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 1 and 3/4 cups (350g) granulated sugar
  • 5 large egg whites, at room temperature
  • 1/3 cup (80g) full-fat sour cream or plain Greek yogurt, at room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup (120g/ml) whole milk, at room temperature
  • 1/2 cup reduced strawberry purée (from step 1)
  • Optional: 1–2 drops red or pink food coloring
    • STRAWBERRY CREAM CHEESE FROSTING
  • 1 cup (about 25g) freeze-dried strawberries
  • 8 ounces (226g) full-fat brick cream cheese, softened to room temperature
  • 1/2 cup (8 Tbsp; 113g) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 3 cups (360g) confectioners’ sugar
  • 1 Tablespoon whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt, to taste

INSTRUCTIONS

    PUREE
  1. Make the reduced strawberry puree: In a blender or food processor, puree the fresh strawberries. You should have a little more than 1 cup (about 270g). Transfer to a small saucepan and simmer over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally, until reduced to 1/2 cup (about 135g), about 25–35 minutes. Cool completely. For best results, refrigerate overnight and bring to room temperature before using.
    2. CAKE:
  2. Preheat the oven to 350°F (177°C). Grease two 9-inch round cake pans, line with parchment paper rounds, then grease the parchment.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together the cake flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  4. In a large bowl using a handheld or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the butter and sugar together on medium-high speed until light and creamy, about 3 minutes.
  5. Add the egg whites and beat on high speed until combined, about 2 minutes. Scrape down the bowl as needed.
  6. Add the sour cream and vanilla extract. Beat on medium-high speed until combined, about 1 minute.
  7. Add the dry ingredients. With the mixer on low speed, slowly pour in the milk. Mix until just combined—do not overmix.
  8. Whisk in the room-temperature reduced strawberry puree and food coloring (if using). Ensure no lumps remain. The batter should be slightly thick.
  9. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and smooth the tops.
  10. Bake for 24–25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  11. Cool the cakes in the pans on a rack for 1 hour. Run a knife around the edges, remove the cakes, peel off parchment, and allow to cool completely before frosting.
    12. CREAM CHEESE FROSTING:
  12. Process the freeze-dried strawberries in a blender or food processor until a fine powder forms. Sift to remove any larger pieces.
  13. In a large bowl using a handheld or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the cream cheese and butter together on medium-high speed until smooth, about 2 minutes.
  14. Add the confectioners’ sugar, strawberry powder, milk, and vanilla. Beat on low for 30 seconds, then on high for 3 minutes until creamy. Add a pinch of salt if needed.
  15. Cover and refrigerate the frosting for 1 hour before using. Yields about 3 cups (720g) of frosting.
    16. ASSEMBLY:
  16. Level the cake layers: using a serrated knife, slice a thin layer off the tops to create flat surfaces.
  17. Place the first layer on a cake stand or turntable. Spread 3/4–1 cup (180–240g) of frosting evenly on top.
  18. Place the second layer upside-down on top. Spread 1 cup (240g) of frosting over the top and sides to create a thin crumb coat. Smooth with a bench scraper.
  19. Refrigerate the cake for about 20 minutes until the crumb coat sets.
  20. Cover the top and sides with the remaining frosting. For best slicing, refrigerate at least 20 minutes before cutting.
  21. Store leftover cake tightly covered in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. If chilled longer than 4 hours, bring to room temperature for about 2 hours before serving.
