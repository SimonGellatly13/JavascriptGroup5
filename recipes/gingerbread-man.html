Gingerbread Man Cookies

These Gingerbread Men are the ultimate Christmas cookies! My recipe yields soft, yet firm gingerbread cookies that are perfectly spiced, buttery, and hold their shape beautifully. Top them off with a foolproof icing that makes plenty for decorating, and you've got yourself a fun holiday activity for the whole family.

INGREDIENTS

    GINGERBREAD DOUGH
  • 1/2 cup (113 g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup (100 g) light or dark brown sugar, tightly packed
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) unsulphured molasses (Grandma's or Brer Rabbit recommended; avoid blackstrap)
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 3/4 cups (345 g) all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
    • ICING
  • 3 cups (375 g) powdered sugar, sifted
  • 2–4 tablespoons milk
  • 2 tablespoons light corn syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Decorative candies and sprinkles, as desired

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Combine the butter, sugar, and molasses. Use an electric mixer or stand mixer with a paddle attachment to beat until the mixture is creamy and well combined.
  2. Stir in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined. The mixture may look separated or curdled — this is normal and will come together once the dry ingredients are added.
  3. In a separate medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, salt, and spices (ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg).
  4. With the mixer on low speed, gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients until the dough is smooth, cohesive, and fully combined.
  5. Divide the dough into two parts (it will be sticky; use a spatula), wrap each portion tightly in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 3 hours or overnight.
  6. Once the dough has chilled, preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or use an ungreased baking sheet.
  7. Generously flour a clean work surface and lightly flour the dough as well.
  8. Use a rolling pin to roll the dough to about 1/4 inch (0.6 cm) thickness. Use a cookie cutter (about 3.5 inches long) to cut out shapes.
  9. Use a spatula to transfer the cookies to the prepared baking sheet, spacing them at least 2 inches apart.
  10. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 8–10 minutes.
  11. Allow the cookies to cool completely on the baking sheet before decorating.
  12. To make the icing: Combine powdered sugar, 2 tablespoons of milk, corn syrup, and vanilla extract in a medium bowl and stir until smooth.
  13. Check the consistency: the icing should drizzle off the spoon and hold its shape for a few seconds before blending back into the bowl. Add more milk (1 teaspoon at a time) if too thick, or more powdered sugar if too thin.
  14. Transfer the icing to a piping bag fitted with a small round tip (such as Wilton #5), or place in a zip-top bag and snip off a tiny corner.
  15. Pipe the icing onto the cooled cookies and add decorative candies or sprinkles as desired.
  16. Allow the frosting to harden before serving (usually about 30 minutes, longer if icing is thinner).
  17. Store uneaten cookies in an airtight container at room temperature.
